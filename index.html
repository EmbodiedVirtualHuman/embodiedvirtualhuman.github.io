Abstract

Building virtual humans requires more than just realistic appearances and diverse movements; it necessitates simulating the complex interplay between internal cognitive states and external environments, as described by the concept of embodied cognition. In this paper, we propose a cognitive architecture that captures this interaction by integrating key mental states—value, belief, desire, and intention—to drive the virtual human’s behavior within an interactive 3D environment. To enable meaningful embodiment, we introduce two novel interactive modules: the action execution module, which translates internal intentions into embodied movements, facilitating rich physical interactions, and the exploration module, which allows the agent to actively explore its environment and update its mental states through feedback. Our approach allows virtual humans to continuously adapt, learn, and evolve their behavior in response to the environment, supporting dynamic and natural human-like interactions. Extensive experiments demonstrate the flexibility and scalability of our method in simulating individualized, daily-level behaviors in unknown environments.

Method overview

Framework of our embodied cognition-aware virtual human. To simulate a truly ``virtual human" in an unfamiliar environment, we design a cognitive architecture composed of value, belief, desire, and intention. Starting from the value (virtual human profile) and the belief (dynamic scene graph), we leverage an LLM to generate the desires of the virtual human in the moment, which are translated into concrete intentions. Two interactive modules are proposed to enable embodied action execution and dynamic environment exploration.
